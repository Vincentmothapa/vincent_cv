Design of Loss of Grid Protection Scheme for Microgrid Clusters Integrated to Distribution Level in a Utility Network

This was my final year project. The project is about the design of a protection scheme for a microgrid cluster that is connected to the utility grid. The protection scheme could be either voltage based or frequency based. Literature was reviewed concerning the various options there are for protecting microgrids and a frequency based scheme was selected to this setup. The frequency based scheme selected uses the rate of change of frequency (ROCOF) relay.

The system was put together that would be used to simulate the faults that were to be used for testing the ROCOF relay. The microgrid cluster consists of 2 microgrids that are powered by wind and PV based distributed generators respectively. Suitable components for the network system were selected. Three case studies were considered:
  • Case 1: Single line-to-ground fault
  • Case 2: 3-phase fault
  • Case 3: Microgrid fault

The above cases were selected because they are the most common fault type and the most severe fault type respectively. The last case was a fault on one of the microgrids. The designed ROCOF relay managed to detect all the fault types and successfully trigger the CBs to open.
